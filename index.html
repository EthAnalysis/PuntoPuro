Company Introduction

Punto Puro was founded by Albert Anelli, whose passion for bocce ball began during childhood family gatherings in northeast Pennsylvania. With deep Italian roots and decades of competitive experience—including international play in Italy and Brazil—Mr. Anelli brought his engineering expertise to redesign the modern bocce ball. A former aerospace engineer, he saw the need for a ball that combined low friction, durability, and balance for consistent performance.

From the family-run Anelli Bocce Club in Peckville, PA, to prestigious tournaments across the globe, Punto Puro reflects Mr. Anelli's lifelong dedication to the sport and its players.

Problems of Competing Designs

  • Inconsistent Balance: Multi-piece designs often feature uneven coatings, causing the center of mass to shift from the true center, leading to balls that curve unpredictably.
  • Durability Issues: Thin surface coatings on cheaper balls can crack or chip, rendering the set unusable after a single game.
  • Lack of Precision: Inferior roundness, sphericity, and weight tolerances result in poor pointing and raffa performance.

How Punto Puro Solves These Problems Best

  • Unmatched Precision: Precision molds, turning tools, and finishing techniques ensure flawless roundness and sphericity, second to none.
  • Tightest Weight Tolerances: With a weight tolerance of only ±2.5 grams within a set, Punto Puro offers the most consistent performance in the world.
  • Solid One-Piece Construction: Made of solid premium resin, these balls maintain a perfectly centered mass for straight rolls.
  • Durability: The solid construction prevents cracking and chipping, even during intense play, ensuring longevity.
  • Visual Feedback: Marbleized finishes help players assess speed and deceleration, aiding hand-eye coordination and game adjustments.
  • Superior Surface Finish: Low-friction surfaces support backspin and precise raffa "stick" shots, elevating advanced play.
